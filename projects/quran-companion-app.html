Overview

The active product combines five main areas in one shell: Quran browsing and recitation practice, memorization mode, prayer times, qibla compass, and dhikr tracking. It launches through a splash screen and then moves into a five-tab shell: Home, Quran, Zikir, Prayer, and Settings.

The Quran flow supports surah browsing, Arabic and English search, ayah reading, recitation audio, and microphone-based recitation practice. Recitation is evaluated through speech capture, Quran-aware post-processing, and comparison logic with lenient, strict Arabic, and tajweed-style modes.

The app has also grown into a broader daily-use Islamic utility with prayer times, qibla direction, live compass support, dhikr counters, reminders, localization, and polished mobile UI.

Core Features

A single app that blends Quran practice with daily Islamic utilities.

Quran library

Browse surahs, search by number or Arabic/English names, open ayahs, and play recitation audio.

Recitation evaluation

Arabic speech is post-processed and compared to target ayahs with multiple comparison modes.

Memorization mode

Words are progressively revealed as the user recites correctly, creating a guided memorization flow.

Prayer times

The app shows today’s schedule, next prayer, live countdown, and supports local notifications.

Qibla compass

The prayer experience includes qibla bearing plus a live compass screen using device heading data.

Dhikr and tasbih

Preset and custom dhikr sets, vibration feedback, persistence, and daily reminder support are built in.

Architecture

Provider-based state with top-level controllers for app settings, Quran practice, prayer times, and zikir.

Data path

Quran text is local-first through bundled asset JSON, with local notifications and custom localization instead of generated ARB.

Important gaps

Some legacy API/SQLite services remain in the repo, and remote tajweed verification is not fully wired into the live flow.

Localization and UI

Custom string tables, Kurdish-first language behavior, RTL switching, Material 3 theming, gradients, and mobile-first screens shape the app experience.

Audio and notifications

Audio can come from bundled assets or remote URLs, while reminders are handled through local notification services.

Repo observations

The README reflects an older architecture, while the active app is centered on asset-driven Quran content and provider-based controllers.
